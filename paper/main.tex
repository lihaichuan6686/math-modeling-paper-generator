\begin{abstract}
This draft demonstrates the v1.0 mathematical modeling paper-generation workflow on a synthetic production-route E problem. The problem is decomposed into representative material screening, short-term demand forecasting, rolling production planning, service-level evaluation, and capacity-scenario comparison. First, three representative materials are summarized and their short-term forecasts are generated by a weighted rolling rule. Second, a rolling production policy transforms the forecasts into weekly production quantities under inventory and safety-stock constraints. Third, code-generated tables and figures report forecast checks, production plans, service levels, and scenario differences between two capacity-use assumptions. Under the baseline scenario, the average service level remains above 95\% across the three representative materials, and the higher-capacity scenario improves or matches the baseline service level for all materials. The numerical outputs are synthetic and are used only to verify the production-route E toolchain rather than support a real contest conclusion.

\keywords{mathematical modeling\quad production-route E\quad rolling production\quad service level\quad reproducibility}
\end{abstract}
