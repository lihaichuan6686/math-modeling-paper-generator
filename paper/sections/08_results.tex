Table~\ref{tab:demo-v1-production-plan} gives the rolling production plan for scenario $k_1$. This is the main executable artifact of the paper: every row corresponds to one material-week decision with forecast demand, production quantity, ending inventory, and service level.

\input{tables/demo_v1_production_plan.tex}

Table~\ref{tab:demo-v1-service-levels} summarizes the average forecast demand, average production, minimum inventory, and average service level under both scenarios. It plays the same structural role as the service-level tables in the production-route E papers: the reader can judge whether the route is safe, not only whether it is mathematically neat.

\input{tables/demo_v1_service_levels.tex}

Figure~\ref{fig:demo-v1-service} visualizes the average service level under scenario $k_1$. All three materials remain near or above the 95\% target, which is the key success metric for this demo.

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{figures/demo_v1_service_levels.png}
\caption{Average service level under the baseline capacity scenario $k_1$.}
\label{fig:demo-v1-service}
\end{figure}

The combined interpretation of the forecast table, rolling production table, and service-level summary is that the demo now behaves like a small production-route E paper. The route is still lightweight, but forecasting no longer stops at a curve: it directly feeds service-level and production decisions.
