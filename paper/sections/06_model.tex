\section{Model Establishment}

\subsection{Short-Term Demand Forecast}

For each representative material, the next-period demand is predicted by a weighted rolling average:

\begin{equation}
\hat d_{t+1}=0.5d_t+0.3d_{t-1}+0.2d_{t-2}.
\end{equation}

The purpose of this forecast is not to demonstrate the best possible time-series method. Instead, it gives the production rule a reproducible short-term signal that can be checked against recent actual values. In a stronger production-route E paper, this layer could be replaced by ARIMA, exponential smoothing, or a mixed forecast-comparison section like the one observed in `2022 E029`.

\subsection{Rolling Production Rule}

Let $I_t$ denote ending inventory, $p_t$ denote the production quantity, $d_t$ denote forecast demand, and $I^{safe}$ denote safety stock. The rolling inventory balance is:

\begin{equation}
I_t = I_{t-1}+p_t-d_t.
\end{equation}

The production quantity depends on the forecast-inventory gap:

\begin{equation}
g_t=d_t+I^{safe}-I_{t-1}.
\end{equation}

The demo uses a simple interpretable control coefficient $\alpha_t$ and computes:

\begin{equation}
p_t=\max(0,\min(C,\alpha_t g_t)),
\end{equation}

where $C$ is the scenario capacity. The coefficient $\alpha_t$ is selected from a small banded rule: when the gap is large, the rule reacts aggressively; when the gap is small or negative, the rule becomes conservative. This design intentionally imitates the service-level-first flavor seen in `2022 E014`.

\subsection{Scenario Comparison}

Two capacity scenarios are considered:

\begin{enumerate}
\item scenario $k_1$: baseline production capacity;
\item scenario $k_2$: expanded production capacity.
\end{enumerate}

The comparison is not framed as a full optimization contest between many policies. It is a compact route-specific validation layer: if the stronger capacity scenario improves service or inventory robustness, the paper can explain why capacity assumptions matter.

\subsection{Validation Targets}

The route is checked against three targets:

\begin{enumerate}
\item forecast usefulness: the short-term forecast should be auditable against recent actual demand;
\item support ability: average service level should remain high for representative materials;
\item feasibility: minimum inventory should stay non-negative under the rolling rule.
\end{enumerate}

These validation targets match the production-route E contract: forecasting must flow into service-level and inventory consequences rather than remain an isolated accuracy exercise.
