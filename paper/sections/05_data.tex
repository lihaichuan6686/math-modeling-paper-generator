\section{Data Processing}

The demo data are synthetic and are stored directly in the generation script. The purpose is not to mimic a full contest attachment, but to exercise a production-route E chain:

\begin{equation}
\text{historical demand} \rightarrow \text{forecast} \rightarrow \text{rolling production} \rightarrow \text{service-level audit}.
\end{equation}

Three representative material groups are retained for the demo. Their historical demand, current inventory, safety stock, and two capacity assumptions are summarized in Table~\ref{tab:demo-v1-material-summary}. This is the same structural move seen in the production-route E papers: the paper does not attempt to plan every possible item first, but works with a reduced set of representative materials.

\input{tables/demo_v1_material_summary.tex}

The data checks used in this demo are deliberately lightweight:

\begin{enumerate}
\item all historical demand values must be positive;
\item current inventory and safety stock must be non-negative;
\item scenario capacities must be at least as large as zero;
\item units are treated as consistent across demand, production, and inventory.
\end{enumerate}

For a real contest problem, the data section would also include missing-value repair, abnormal order filtering, time-scale conversion, and material grouping evidence from attachments. For this v1.0 production-route E demo, the preprocessing focus is simpler: identify representative materials and convert them into forecast and production inputs.
