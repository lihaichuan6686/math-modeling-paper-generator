# Completion Gate

Purpose: define when a generated CUMCM-style draft is complete enough to hand off for research review.

This is a closure checklist, not a style guide.

## Required Closure

A draft is not complete unless all of the following are true:

1. The problem map is stable from abstract to conclusion.
2. Each subquestion has a model, a result, and a validation path.
3. Every important result in the body can be traced to data, code, equations, or documented schematic sources.
4. The paper has at least one visible artifact per major subquestion.
5. The PDF compiles and the rendered pages are readable.
6. Tables and figures have captions, labels, and text references.
7. The artifact ledger lists the outputs that support the paper claims.
8. The review file names the remaining risks instead of pretending they do not exist.
9. Responsible-use limits are stated clearly.

## Family-Specific Closure

### Evaluation and Ranking

- ranking exists;
- sensitivity or stability check exists;
- final recommendation is not just the ranking table.

### Evaluation to Planning

- ranking feeds the plan;
- plan is executable;
- feasibility or capacity audit exists.

### Forecast to Decision

- forecast is evaluated out of sample or by a clear check;
- uncertainty is propagated into the decision;
- scenario or stress test exists.

### Classification and Recognition

- preprocessing and feature logic are documented;
- model comparison exists;
- confusion-level or error-case evidence exists.

### Geometry and Engineering Mechanics

- the object or coordinate frame is shown early;
- the math closes to a physical or engineering metric;
- residual or feasibility evidence exists.

### Online Optimization and Process Update

- baseline and updated plan both appear;
- the trigger or update rule is explained;
- before/after comparison exists.

## Hard Stops

Do not mark a run complete if any of these are true:

- the paper only has method names without outputs;
- the paper only looks long because of screenshots, blank pages, or residue pages;
- the body has unsupported numbers;
- key artifacts exist only in appendix form;
- the route family is still ambiguous;
- the PDF is not verified.

## Hand-off Rule

When the gate passes, the next hand-off should include:

- problem-analysis file;
- model-plan file;
- figure-plan file;
- artifact ledger;
- review note;
- compiled PDF or compile status.
