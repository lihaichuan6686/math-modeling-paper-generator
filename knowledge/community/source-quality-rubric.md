# Source Quality Rubric For Community Material

Purpose: help Claude Code use online mathematical-modeling material without copying weak or unsafe content.

## Source Classes

### High Quality

Use confidently as process guidance when consistent with the problem.

Examples:

- official contest documents;
- university training notes;
- detailed veteran reflections with reasoning;
- public excellent-paper analyses;
- posts that show assumptions, method, evidence, and caveats.

### Medium Quality

Use as a signal, not authority.

Examples:

- detailed Bilibili explanation videos;
- long-form Zhihu answers;
- public blog solution summaries;
- Xiaohongshu experience posts with concrete workflow advice.

### Low Quality

Use only as weak signal.

Examples:

- short answer-only posts;
- screenshots without context;
- comments that only list a final number;
- "must use this algorithm" claims without reasoning.

### Reject

Do not use.

Examples:

- paid leaked answers;
- private group materials;
- content encouraging rule violations;
- final-answer dumps with no method;
- material that asks for or offers disguised authorship.

## Extraction Rule

Extract:

- soft rules;
- common traps;
- rough consensus;
- possible validation ideas;
- presentation patterns.

Do not extract:

- full solution text;
- exact answer tables unless public and used only for sanity range;
- private materials;
- paid course content.

## Citation And Trace Rule

In a run note, record:

- search query;
- platform;
- source title or short description;
- source-quality class;
- extracted signal;
- whether it changed the route.

If a source cannot be revisited later, mark it as weak evidence.
