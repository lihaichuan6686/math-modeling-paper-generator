# Rail Timetable Demo Problem

This is a synthetic research-only demo for the Type I rail transit timetable route. It is not a real contest problem and not contest evidence.

Given a 12-station urban rail line, one-hour peak section passenger flows, train capacity, headway limits, station dwell limits, and a minimum tracking interval:

1. Determine a large/small route operation plan that balances operating cost and passenger service level.
2. Generate an equal-interval timetable and provide arrival/departure times at served stations.
3. Audit section capacity, headway/tracking interval, and dwell-time feasibility.
4. Give operating recommendations under demand and headway scenarios.

Required outputs:

- operation-plan candidate table;
- selected operation plan;
- full timetable;
- capacity audit;
- tracking and dwell audit;
- scenario analysis;
- section-flow, cost-service, and timetable figures.
