# Writing Style Plan

## Style Target

- target version: `v1.2`
- route family: `monitoring-route E`
- selected style variant: `route-heavy analytical + evidence-dense research style`
- positive sample: `official-paper paradigms for E-route monitoring style`
- contrast sample: `official-style-vs-modern-draft-risk.md`

## Core Writing Choices

- abstract density target: `one dense page with diagnosis -> policy -> consequence closure`
- paragraph-driven vs bullet-driven balance: `paragraph-driven in body; policy details moved into tables`
- tone target: `measured, diagnostic, policy-aware`
- how results will be interpreted in prose: `every diagnostic artifact should say what policy or monitoring implication it has`
- how limitations will be stated: `state horizon boundary, threshold sensitivity, and intervention scope directly`
- family-specific abstract skeleton: `diagnostic finding -> policy or monitoring rule -> intervention or long-horizon validation`
- preferred subquestion close-out pattern: `diagnostic/policy result -> practical meaning -> comparison confirmation -> next-step transition`
- family-specific density cue: `the diagnosis-to-policy bridge, rule-construction paragraph, and consequence-interpretation paragraph must be heavier than descriptive diagnostic recap`

## Section Notes

| Section | Intended feel | What must not happen |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Abstract | closes diagnostics and policy together | pattern-only summary |
| Problem analysis | explains why detection must become policy | descriptive process retelling |
| Model establishment | diagnosis layer and policy layer are both explicit | policy rule hidden in loose prose |
| Results | policy artifact and consequence artifact are both interpreted | diagnostic artifact stacking |
| Validation | intervention or long-horizon language stays visible | token `effectiveness` claim |
| Conclusion | ends with rule/policy plus scope boundary | detected-pattern-only ending |

## Paragraph Rhythm

- preferred rhythm: `diagnostic fact -> trigger meaning -> policy implication -> consequence transition`
- maximum tolerance for stacked micro-paragraphs: `avoid long runs of descriptive diagnostic sentences`
- list-risk zones: `results and conclusion`
- required interpretation zones: `after diagnostic artifact`, `after policy artifact`, `after intervention or long-horizon artifact`
- family-specific bridge paragraphs that must exist: `diagnosis-to-policy paragraph`, `policy-to-consequence paragraph`
- section most likely to become artifact-stacked: `results`
- section that should stay intentionally compact: `problem restatement`

## Human-Team Soft Rules

- diagnostics should sound like the reason for the policy, not the final answer;
- the chosen policy should be justified in terms of trigger logic and consequence evidence;
- each subquestion should end by stating what monitoring or intervention conclusion is now defensible;
- recommendation language should stay inside the studied horizon and tested scenarios.

## Thinness Risks

- sections likely to become too short: `policy interpretation`
- sections likely to become too bullet-heavy: `results`
- sections likely to overuse generic prose: `conclusion`
- subquestions most likely to end without a real close-out sentence: `diagnostic subsection`
