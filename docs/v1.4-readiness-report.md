## What v1.4 Does Not Prove

v1.4 does not yet prove:

- the generated paper has award-level methods;
- every problem family is equally strong;
- public-community search will be available for every topic;
- every generated LaTeX paper will compile without local environment issues;
- the paper will be 30-45 pages without further problem-specific work;
- the result is suitable for active contest submission.

v1.4 should be judged by user-side practice tests, not by static documentation alone.

## Test Success Criteria

A good v1.4 test run should show:

- a dense abstract with method-result closure for every major subquestion;
- a problem profile and route decision before modeling;
- coordinated modeling, programming, and writing evidence in run files;
- an online-consensus note or explicit skip reason;
- Playwright MCP public browsing attempts or access-limit notes when public-web checking is available;
- body-visible result tables and figures;
- validation or comparison artifacts when the route supports them;
- section-budget and writing-style plans that reflect the generated paper;
- a review that catches paper-feel, evidence, and online-consensus failures;
- final answers aligned across abstract, body, conclusion, and artifact ledger.

## Fast Failure Signals

Treat the run as a v1.4 failure if:

- the abstract is short, generic, or method-only;
- public discussion is copied or popularity is treated as proof;
- figures/tables are decorative or disconnected from subquestions;
- final answers appear only in appendix or code output;
- assumptions, symbols, evaluation, or references are ceremonial;
- the review misses obvious missing answers or unsupported claims;
- the paper reaches length through background padding or appendix inflation.

## Next Feedback Targets

After the next user-side test, prioritize repairs in this order:

1. abstract/result/ledger inconsistency;
2. missing or weak body result artifacts;
3. shallow method chains without validation;
4. online-consensus notes that do not change run files or reject signals;
5. page rhythm failures in the first 12 pages;
6. section-duty failures such as unused assumptions or decorative symbol tables;
7. machine-like prose and repetitive paragraph endings.
