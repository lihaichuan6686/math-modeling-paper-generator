\begin{abstract}
To solve the supplier screening and executable planning problem, we first construct a multi-indicator evaluation system for candidate suppliers and transform heterogeneous information such as price, reliability, capacity, and loss rate into a comparable score structure. On this basis, a planning model is established to determine the period-by-period ordering or allocation quantities under demand, capacity, and service constraints. The resulting plan no longer stops at ranking, but gives a directly executable decision table together with the selected candidate set. Furthermore, feasibility audits and scenario comparisons are introduced to examine whether the recommendation remains credible under demand growth and supply-side disturbance. The final route therefore closes the full chain of score generation, candidate selection, executable planning, and robustness checking, and provides a practical recommendation that can be handed over for further human review.

\keywords{mathematical modeling\quad evaluation to planning\quad supplier screening\quad feasibility audit\quad scenario comparison}
\end{abstract}
